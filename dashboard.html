Connectivity Issues - Computers

What the Issues Look Like

  • Pages not loading or stuck on a loading screen.
  • Errors such as "This site can't be reached" or "Connection timed out."
  • Inability to log in despite correct credentials.
  • Portal appears blank or only partially loads.
  • Buttons unresponsive or very slow to react.

Common Checks & Solutions

1

Check WiFi Connection

Is the computer connected to a WiFi network?

Connected Limited Disconnected
2

Restart Router

Power cycle the router and reattempt connection:

  1. Unplug the router from power
  2. Wait 30 seconds
  3. Plug it back in
  4. Wait 2 minutes for full restart
3

Try Another Site

Test if other websites (e.g., www.google.com) are working to rule out general internet outage.

4

Browser Issues

5

Firewall/Antivirus Settings

Ensure they aren't blocking the portal/LS URLs.

6

VPNs

Turn off any VPNs that may interfere with the connection.

Clues It's a Local Issue

  • Other sites also fail to load.
  • Only one user or one machine is affected.
  • Issues resolve with a browser switch or WiFi change.
  • Mobile phone hotspot works when WiFi does not.

Connectivity Issues - Phones

What the Issues Look Like

  • App or webapp won't open or gets stuck on the loading screen.
  • Error messages like "No internet connection" or "Couldn't load data."
  • Delay in uploading results or recordings.
  • Buttons appear greyed out or unresponsive.

Common Checks & Solutions

1

WiFi/Data Toggle

Switch between WiFi and mobile data to see which works better.

WiFi Mobile Data
2

Airplane Mode

Toggle it on and off to reset network settings.

  1. Turn on Airplane Mode for 10 seconds
  2. Turn off Airplane Mode
  3. Wait for connection to reestablish
3

App Refresh

Force-close the app and reopen.

On most phones: Swipe up from bottom to see recent apps, then swipe away the app to close it.

4

Update Browser

Ensure the default browser (e.g., Chrome or Safari) is up to date.

5

Clear Mobile Browser Cache

How to clear cache in Chrome (tap to expand)
  1. Open Chrome app
  2. Tap the three dots (⋮) in the top right
  3. Select "Settings"
  4. Tap "Privacy and Security"
  5. Select "Clear browsing data"
  6. Choose "Cached images and files"
  7. Tap "Clear data"
How to clear cache in Safari (tap to expand)
  1. Open Settings app
  2. Scroll down and tap "Safari"
  3. Scroll down and tap "Clear History and Website Data"
  4. Confirm by tapping "Clear"
6

Try Another Network

e.g., home WiFi or hotspot from another device.

7

Restart the Phone

A full device restart can resolve many connection issues.

Clues It's a Local Issue

  • Works fine on a different WiFi or mobile network.
  • Other apps also struggling to connect.
  • Device hasn't been restarted in a while.
  • Issue is isolated to one user or device.

Issues That Should Be Escalated

These issues are not connectivity-related and require specialist support.

Types of Issues

  • Login credentials not working despite verified details.
  • Unexpected app behavior (e.g., scoring errors, broken audio, data not saving).
  • Missing or incomplete pupil data despite proper setup.
  • Persistent issues across multiple devices or users.
  • App crashes or bugs even with strong internet connection.

Common Checks Before Escalating

Who to Escalate To

Contact the support team via slack/email.

Include:

  • Description of the issue.
  • Device type and browser/app used.
  • Screenshots or error messages if possible.
  • Steps already taken to troubleshoot.

Internet Speed Explained

What Is Internet Speed?

Internet speed is how fast data moves between your device (like a phone or laptop) and the internet. It's measured in Mbps - Megabits per second.

The median mobile internet download speed via cellular data networks is 19.23 Mbps as of January 2025 in Paraguay.

Slow Internet

0–10 Mbps

Web browsing

Works okay for simple websites but may take time to load image-heavy pages.

Streaming

You can watch YouTube or Netflix, but only in standard definition (SD). You'll often see buffering.

Video calls

Choppy or blurry on Zoom or FaceTime, especially if more than one person is on the internet.

Downloading a movie

Could take over an hour.

Feels like: Walking in mud - you'll get there, but slowly and with frustration.

Medium Speed

10–100 Mbps

Web browsing

Fast and smooth.

Streaming

You can watch in HD (High Definition) without issues. Maybe even 4K on the higher end.

Video calls

Clear and stable, even with multiple people.

Downloading a movie
